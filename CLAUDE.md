# Purpose & context

Caleb works on content for Stoa, a Stoic meditation app, developing courses, routines, and meditations that blend Stoic philosophy with guided practice. His work spans multiple content types: introductory onboarding courses for new users, re-engagement courses for lapsed practitioners, and topical courses on subjects like addiction. A consistent goal across projects is creating content that is both philosophically substantive and practically accessible—meeting users at real moments of need.

Key content benchmarks: Waking Up is a reference point for balancing scientific grounding with practical philosophy instruction. The Stoa content library includes a catalog of quotes from Marcus Aurelius, Epictetus, Seneca, and others, each with structured IDs (quote IDs and sentence IDs) that must be preserved precisely in outputs.

Stoa's house voice is plain, measured, and direct—explicitly not motivational, punchy, or inspirational in tone.

# Key learnings & principles

- Content ideas should lead with acquisition and conversion value, not philosophical completeness. The strongest meditation topics target high-intent moments—acute pain points that drive downloads and paid conversions (e.g., job loss, relationship endings, pre-performance anxiety)—not gaps in thematic coverage.
- Tone enforcement is a recurring priority. Caleb consistently catches language that drifts toward motivational or colloquial registers and resets it to Stoa's plain, measured voice.
- Explicit benefit language matters. Implications are not enough; courses should name what practitioners gain, not just describe the process.
- Closings should honor accomplishment before pointing forward. Endings that immediately pivot to "what's next" undercut the sense of completion.
- Missing rationale is a flag. When practitioners are given options (e.g., eyes open or closed), the "why" needs to be present, not assumed.

# Approach & patterns

- Caleb prefers an outline-first workflow: drafts are approved at the outline stage before full composition begins.
- Courses are built with explicit thematic arcs—each day connects to a named skill or concept progression, not just standalone lessons.
- Timing is tracked carefully for guided meditations: narration word counts at guided pace plus pause durations are calculated to estimate total runtime.
- Caleb iterates with direct, specific pushback rather than broad redirection—he names the exact phrase or structural issue and explains why it misses the mark.
