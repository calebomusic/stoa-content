# Routine Paradigms

Reference exemplars for writing new Stoa routines. Model new content on these files.

## House structure

Use this beat order. Don't deviate without reason.

1. **Hook** — a question, image, or claim that earns the listener's attention in one or two sentences.
2. **Quote** — primary source, introduced with context. Don't lead cold.
3. **Theory** — unpack the quote into a teachable idea. Be willing to take a position.
4. **Meditation** — silence cues (e.g. `20`, `30` for seconds) embedded in the script. The meditation should *enact* the theory, not be a generic relaxation tail.
5. **Commitment** — a sentence the listener affirms.
6. **Action** — one concrete thing to carry into the day.

Close by calling back to the opening quote.

## House style

- **Earn opinions.** The strongest routines are willing to say something pointed. Avoid hedged, generic-mindfulness phrasing.
- **Acknowledge the listener's reality** before prescribing — especially on heavy themes (grief, failure, lapsed practice).
- **Posture-as-disposition cues** beat generic "find a comfortable position." (e.g. "Sit like someone who is ready to act.")
- **Quote integration**: introduce, contextualize, then quote. Don't drop quotes cold into the body. Pull quotes from `resources/quotes/quotes.md` and cite the `Quote ID` so they link cleanly into the app. Vary the openings across a series — the library leans heavily on quotes starting with "It is...", "We...", and "When...", and stacking those reads as monotone.
- **Faithfulness**: cite primary sources (Epictetus, Marcus, Seneca, Musonius, Stobaeus). Use the actual Stoic taxonomy, not folk-ethics glosses.
- **Concrete vignettes** beat abstract examples (the bike-mechanic moment in Justice; Hugh Thompson at My Lai for courage).

## Paradigm exemplars

Top files to model new routines on.

1. **`final/return-to-practice.md`** — The single best exemplar. Self-aware voice ("Even Marcus Aurelius fell off track"). The meditation enacts the theory: mind-wanders-and-returns becomes a meta-lesson on lapsing and resuming the practice itself.

2. **`final/dichotomy-of-control-routines-doc.md`** — Paradigm for **single-concept deep dive**. A complete 7-part arc (Intro → Choice → Acceptance → Identity → Questions → Attention → Mantra) covering one doctrine from multiple angles. The Identity entry reframes DoC as an account of what we *are*, not just a coping tool.

3. **`final/stoa-conent-high-performance-stoicism.md`** — Best use of primary sources. Epictetus on Olympians + Cato's "we'll deserve it" sharpen the distinction between caring about externals vs. demanding the best of what's up to you. Note the posture cue: "Sit like someone who is ready to act."

4. **`final/justice-routines.md`** — Canonical **virtue-series template**. Best lede in the corpus (the trio of "If you promise…" / "If someone asks…" / "How should we respond to injustice…"). Cites Stobaeus, draws on Marcus's cosmopolis, breaks justice into the actual Stoic sub-virtues.

5. **`final/basics-of-stoic-meditatation-routines.md`** — Best onboarding model. Hook ("What if most of your stress was optional?") and explicit differentiation from Buddhist/therapeutic meditation: "The goal is not to stop thinking — but to think well."

6. **`final/stoa-content-2026-resilience-v2.md`** — Best short-form intro. Tight, well-paced, with a crystalline closer: "The Stoics didn't promise an easy life. They offered something better: a way to become equal to a hard one."

7. **`final/managing-negative-emotions.md`** — Strongest "view from above" execution and a mature treatment of grief ("We may weep, but we must not wail"). Acknowledges the emotion before applying the philosophy.

8. **`final/memento-mori-daily.md`** — Best treatment of a heavy theme. Reframes memento mori as life-affirming. Anchors each lesson with a *different* breath cue so meditations don't feel copy-pasted.

9. **`final/courage-routines-2024.md`** — **Voice exemplar.** "Anxious people end up in therapy and social media, but reckless people end up in jail or the morgue" is the sharpest line in the corpus. The Aristotelian-mean framing is clean. Note: this file uses an older template (no Commitment/Action) — model **prose voice** on it, not structure. The other three virtues (Justice, Discipline, Wisdom) define the current series template.

## Long-form direction

**`final/routines-3-weeks-iii-2022.md`** — Use as the **theory and arc paradigm** for multi-week series. The 21-day shape (weekly themes Foundations / Resilience / Virtues), the historical anchors (Hugh Thompson at My Lai for courage, the Albert Ellis / CBT bridge), and the choice of doctrines per day are well-judged and worth following.

Caveats:
- The prose is workmanlike compared to the 2024–2025 virtue series. **Don't model voice on it.**
- The final ~140 lines (from ~line 2280) are unfinished outline stubs left in the doc — clean these up before pointing new writers at the file.

## Files to skip as paradigms

- Manifests / title lists: `*-filenames-and-titles.md`
- Outline-only drafts: `amor-fati-routines.md`, most of `stoic-motivation.md`, `marcus-aurelius-routines-2026.md` (single intro lesson)
- Lower-density letter walkthroughs: e.g. `seneca-routines-iv.md` — these read as passage commentary rather than tight standalone routines.
