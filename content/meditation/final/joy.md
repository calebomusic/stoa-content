## **PT: Experience Positive Emotions Now**

PD: These practical Stoic meditations are designed to cultivate happiness from within. Despite common misconceptions, Stoicism isn't about suppressing emotions but rather about experiencing positive ones. With these practices, you'll develop the ability to experience genuine joy regardless of external circumstances.

PS: Finding joy in every moment

## **CT: Finding Joy**

CD: Experience happiness from within.

#### Theory

Stoicism is an energizing and joyful philosophy.

It is a common misconception that Stoics do not feel emotions. In fact, Stoics embrace positive emotions and live happy lives.

For the ancient Stoics, this happiness comes from a recognition of the good in the world and in themself. 

In this course, we’ll practice cultivating that joy through Stoic philosophy, mindfulness meditations, and visualization exercises.

By doing so, we’ll cultivate calm and energizing emotions – Stoic joy.

## **CT: The Present Moment**

CD: Find joy here now.

#### **Theory**

You don’t need anyone else’s permission to be happy.

For the Stoics, joy comes from appreciating the good in the world. How do you do that?

It’s simple. Enjoy the present. Avoid anxious dependence on the future. After all, the present is enough. 

That’s where the good is found.

Value good character and beauty of the world, then you will find joy in your own character and the world in the present moment. 

Happy people who do this exude happiness. Let’s work on joining their ranks.

## **CT: Natural Harmony**

CD: Seize happiness from nature.

#### **Theory**

Nature provides what we need to be happy. All we need to do is seize it. We don’t need to wait for something in the future to happen to us. We don’t need someone else’s permission.

This very fact is energizing. Something to take joy in.

Marcus Aurelius advised himself to find happiness by being in harmony with the universe – something he could work on at any moment.

When done rightly this is an awesome experience – like a musician or athlete. 

How do you do it? Simply by staying in the present and playing your part.

Let’s practice that today.

This is an incredible fact. One which we shouldn’t take for granted.

But we need to seize it. Live in the present. Live by your values. Experience joy.

## CT: Live well

CD: Embrace your life roles.

#### **Theory**

We’re each given roles in this life.

The Stoic Epictetus says that we are like actors in a play:

“Remember that you are an actor in a play of whatever kind the director may choose. If a short one, short; if a long one, long. If he wants you to play a beggar, see that you act even this part naturally; or a cripple, or a ruler, or an ordinary citizen. Your task is to give a good performance of the part that you are assigned.”  
id: 1683

Like any actor we can bring levity and joy to our parts.

But more importantly, we can find joy in the roles we have been given. Moment by moment whatever they are. 

How do you do this? By choosing your roles well (an art in and of itself) and then being excellent in them.

Let’s meditate on doing this today.

## CT: Inner Power

CD: Control what affects your happiness.

#### **Theory**

The modern Stoic Sharon Lebell tells us:

“Your happiness depends on three things, all of which are within your power: your will, your ideas concerning the events in which you are involved, and the use you make of your ideas.”   
id: 318

What does this mean?

The will for her is where you direct your energy. This is the psychological drive to do something, or to avoid something, which we all experience. When offered an invitation by a friend, I might have the will to socialize, where someone else may feel the need to stay at home by themselves.

Our will depends on our opinions. If I think an invitation to socialize is good, I will want to socialize. But if I judge the invitation to be a bad thing I will want to stay home. So our will comes from the ideas we have about events in our life, and whether we judge them as good or bad things. 

Finally, this entire process is within our power, because we have the ability to decide what we think. We all possess the freedom and ability to reflect on our impressions about situations, and make the judgment we think is best. This makes us different from other animals.

Let’s meditate on these ideas today.

## CT: Stoic Attention

CD: Flourish even in challenging circumstances.

#### **Theory**

Marcus Aurelius once wrote: 

“Such as are your habitual thoughts, such also will be the character of your mind; for the soul is dyed by the thoughts. Dye it then with a continuous series of such thoughts as these: for instance, that where a man can live, there he can also live well; now it is possible to live at court, so it must also be possible to live well at court.”   
id: 939

Marcus Aurelius’s point is that the state of our mind is determined by our attention. It is influenced by what we think about and what we focus on. If we focus on bad things, our mind will tend towards producing bad thoughts. If we focus on good realities, our mind will find solutions and positives more easily.

This means that to feel Stoic joy, it is not enough to just limit negative thoughts to reduce suffering. We must be able to turn our attention to the positive in ourselves, in others, and in the world. We must make a consistent effort to dye our minds with positive thoughts, by training our attention towards them.

Marcus did not say just “to live at court is not as bad as I thought”, because that kind of thinking only reduces the negative. Instead he said “it must be possible to live well at court”. This kind of thinking turns the mind towards the positive in any situation. 

Let’s practice this today.

Like a musician who has been trained to notice each instrument in a symphony, the Stoic focuses on, and can identify, the positive in the chaos of living. They train their attention towards opportunities to flourish even when things seem bad.
