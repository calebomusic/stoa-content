# Wake Up

So many emperors have been ruled by others. They have let themselves be lulled to sleep by the praise of others or gone mad with the possibility of power. Marcus Aurelius kept his head, calmly saw things as they were, and acted accordingly.

# Amor Fati

You can cultivate the same attitude. When you do, your life will be guided by fate. The Romans called this amor fati, the love of fate.

Life is short.

Everyone knows it, but very few people apply it.

To truly know something, it’s important to rehearse and practice it. That’s why the Stoics saw contemplation, study, and memorization as essential parts of the philosophical life. 

Epictetus uses the metaphor of keeping principles “on hand.” If our principles are accessible we will be able to apply them when we need them most.

# Superior

All humans can withstand pain and discomfort.

Today, let’s meditate on simple and powerful words from the Stoic Porcia Cato. The daughter of the famous Stoic, Cato the Younger, Porcia lived through a time of upheaval and civil war. She was known for her self-assurance and ability to keep a cool head.

Her ability to endure was grounded in the idea that she was better than the obstacles life presented her. We can take motivation in her example. You are more than your feelings and the events that happen to you.
